Overview

What is Maison Aurore Roster?

Maison Aurore Roster is a daily scheduling tool for luxury retail boutiques. It automatically generates shift assignments for your team across three shifts — Morning, Afternoon, and Closing — and scores each roster against five criteria: skill coverage, VIC client affiliation, gender balance, seniority, and language diversity.

The roster engine runs entirely in your browser. Staff data and history are stored in Supabase; the app deploys on Vercel or Netlify with no server to manage.

This guide covers day-to-day use for store managers. For deployment and Supabase setup, see the README in the repository.

Daily View

Reading your roster

When you open the app, it automatically generates today's roster and displays it. The header shows the store name and the overall roster score for the day.

The shift tabs

Use the tab bar to switch between shifts:

● Morning · 09:00 – 14:00 ● Afternoon · 13:00 – 19:00 ● Closing · 17:00 – 21:00

Each tab shows the staff assigned to that shift. The Summary tab shows all three shift scores side by side plus the overall score.

Staff cards

Each staff card shows the person's name, role, and tags for seniority, languages spoken, and whether they have an active VIC client on this shift. Tap a card to expand it and see full details including their VIC client affiliations, gender, and seniority level.

The overall score

The score pill in the top-right corner shows today's overall roster score out of 100. Colour indicates quality:

VIC coverage panel

When any VIC clients are configured, a panel above the shift tabs shows which advisor is covering each client across Morning, Afternoon, and Closing. A Gap! badge means that shift has no affiliated advisor assigned — consider enabling VIC Max or adjusting the roster manually.

Fatigue alerts

If the engine detects a staff member scheduled across too many consecutive shifts, a fatigue warning banner appears below the header with the person's name and a note on the risk level.

Scoring

How the roster score is calculated

Each shift is scored from 0 – 100 by the engine. The overall day score is the average of all three shifts. Five criteria contribute to the score, each with a configurable weight:

Skill coverage 35%

All required roles are present — at least one Floor Manager, Sr. Stylist, VIC Advisor, and Cashier per shift.

VIC affiliation 25%

Every active VIC client has at least one of their affiliated advisors on shift.

Gender balance 15%

No single gender makes up more than 70% of a shift's team.

Seniority cover 15%

At least one senior staff member or manager is present on every shift.

Language coverage 10%

The team covers a diverse set of languages — ideally at least five across the shift.

Weights are adjustable in the Admin panel under Weights. Moving any slider automatically redistributes the remaining weight across the others so the total always stays at 100%.

VIC Coverage

Managing VIC client coverage

VIC (Very Important Client) coverage is a core feature of the roster engine. Each VIC client can be linked to affiliated advisors — staff whose role qualifies them to serve that client (Floor Managers, Sr. Stylists, VIC Advisors).

Reading the VIC panel

On the roster view, the VIC panel shows one card per client. A coloured chip in each shift column means coverage is confirmed. An empty column means no affiliated advisor is on that shift.

VIC Max mode

The ☆ VIC Max toggle in the top-right of the roster view re-runs the engine with VIC affiliation weighted more heavily. Use this when a high-priority client is visiting and you need to ensure coverage across all three shifts, even if it adjusts other assignments.

VIC Max does not override staff availability or "cannot work" flags. It only shifts how the optimiser prioritises assignments among available staff.

Managing VIC clients

Add, remove, and update VIC clients in the Admin panel under the VIC clients tab. You can set an expected visit date for scheduling awareness and assign or remove affiliated advisors directly from each client card.

Admin Panel

The admin panel

Access the admin panel by tapping the Admin button in the top-right of the roster view. The panel has five tabs:

Staff View, add, edit, and remove staff members. Configure roles, seniority, languages, available shifts, and "must work" / "cannot work" flags for today.
VIC clients Add and manage VIC client profiles. Set expected visit dates and link affiliated advisors to each client.
Weights Adjust the five scoring criteria weights with interactive sliders. Changes are saved to Supabase and apply to all future roster generations.
Roster planner Generate a roster, inspect per-shift scores and constraint violations, and drag staff between shifts before submitting for approval.
Publish Review rosters in the pending queue, approve or reject submissions, and browse the full roster history with status badges.

Staff management

Each staff record includes: name, role, seniority level (Junior / Senior / Manager), gender, languages spoken, available shifts, and a "cannot work today" flag. Use the search bar and role filter to find staff quickly. Edit inline by clicking the edit icon on any row.

Setting Cannot work today on a staff member prevents the engine from assigning them to any shift in today's generation. Remember to clear this flag the following day.

Adjusting weights

On the Weights tab, drag a slider to change a criterion's contribution to the score. The other four sliders adjust automatically so the total stays at 100%. Click Save weights to write the configuration to Supabase. The updated weights take effect the next time a roster is generated.

Publishing

The roster approval workflow

Rosters go through a four-state workflow before they are considered final:

Pending review → Approved → Published
Pending review → Rejected
1

Generate & review

In the Roster Planner tab, generate a roster and review the per-shift constraint scorecards. Resolve any violations flagged in the panel (missing roles, VIC gaps) and adjust assignments if needed.

2

Submit for approval

When satisfied, use the Publish button in the Roster Planner. This saves the roster to Supabase with status Pending review.

3

Approve or reject

A manager opens the Publish tab in the admin panel. Pending rosters appear in the queue. Approve to move the roster to Approved, or reject with a note to send it back.

4

Publish

An approved roster can be published — setting status to Published. Published rosters are locked and retained in history. The full log is visible at the bottom of the Publish tab.

Tips

Getting the most from the roster

Keep staff data current

The engine can only optimise for what it knows. Update language lists when staff develop new language skills, and review the available shifts for each person after any contract changes.

Use "Cannot work today" for ad-hoc absences

For sick days or last-minute unavailability, set the Cannot work today toggle on the staff member in the Admin panel before generating the day's roster. This is separate from their base shift availability and resets with each session.

Review VIC expected visit dates

Setting an expected visit date on a VIC client record helps you notice when a client is due — but the engine uses affiliation, not dates, to determine coverage. Ensure affiliations are current.

Calibrate weights to your boutique

The default weights (Skill 35% · VIC 25% · Gender 15% · Seniority 15% · Language 10%) suit a general luxury boutique. If your store has a particularly multilingual clientele, increase Language coverage. If VIC visits are the highest priority, raise VIC affiliation.

Check the Summary tab before publishing

The Summary tab shows all three shift scores at a glance. If one shift scores significantly lower than the others, use the Roster Planner to investigate the constraint breakdown before submitting.

The roster history is stored in Supabase and never deleted. You can review any previous roster from the Publish tab's history list, including the score and the full assignment payload.
